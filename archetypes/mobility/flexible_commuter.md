# Flexible Commuter Mobility

This person has a work routine but can vary office attendance, remote work, departure times, and errands. Their schedule combines work anchors with flexible discretionary stops.

Typical stops include home, office, supermarkets, gyms, cafes, restaurants, fast food, retail stores, pharmacies, banks, parks, malls, post offices, and social or personal errands.

They usually include a work stop when office attendance is part of the schedule, but may shift the commute earlier or later, add errands near the office, stop for meals, or combine several tasks into one route.

With remote or lighter work obligations, they may stay home longer, make midday trips, visit the gym, run errands, meet someone for a meal, or defer non-urgent stops to a more convenient time.

The schedule should show adaptability while still preserving a plausible work-life rhythm. Prefer stops that fit naturally around work obligations and avoid making the day feel scattered.
