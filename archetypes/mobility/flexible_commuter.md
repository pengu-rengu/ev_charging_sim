# Flexible Commuter Mobility

This person has a work routine but can vary office days, remote days, departure times, and errands. Their schedule combines work anchors with flexible discretionary stops.

Typical stops include home, office, supermarkets, gyms, cafes, restaurants, fast food, retail stores, pharmacies, banks, parks, malls, post offices, and social or personal errands.

On office weekdays, they usually include a work stop but may shift the commute earlier or later, add errands near the office, stop for meals, or combine several tasks into one route.

On remote or lighter workdays, they may stay home longer, make midday trips, visit the gym, run errands, meet someone for a meal, or defer non-urgent stops to a more convenient time.

Weekend travel is discretionary and can include shopping, recreation, meals, social plans, parks, and household errands. The schedule should show adaptability while still preserving a plausible work-life rhythm.
