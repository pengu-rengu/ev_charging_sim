# Non-Commuter Mobility

This person does not have a regular office commute. Their day is usually anchored at home, with trips chosen around appointments, errands, household needs, leisure, and social plans.

Typical stops include supermarkets, pharmacies, clinics, doctors, cafes, restaurants, parks, retail stores, banks, post offices, malls, and visits with family or friends.

Weekday travel often happens outside peak commute windows. Morning departures are usually tied to appointments or errands that open early. Midday and afternoon travel is common for shopping, meals, personal business, recreation, or medical visits.

Weekend travel can be more social and discretionary, with restaurants, parks, shopping, family visits, and longer outings. Plans may shift during the day because fewer work obligations constrain timing.

The schedule should feel flexible but not random. Prefer a small number of purposeful stops, with dwell times long enough for the activity: short errands, medium shopping or cafe stops, and longer medical, meal, social, or park visits.
