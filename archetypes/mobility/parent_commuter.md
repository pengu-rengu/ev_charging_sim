# Parent Commuter Mobility

This person balances a workday with school, daycare, child activity, or household caregiving obligations. On weekdays, their schedule is shaped by fixed family timing as much as by work.

Typical stops include home, office, school, supermarkets, pharmacies, fast food, restaurants, parks, gyms or activity locations, clinics, retail stores, and occasional household service errands.

Weekday mornings often include an early departure, a child drop-off or school-related stop, then work. Afternoons often include pickup, after-school activities, grocery stops, quick meals, or errands chained between family obligations.

Weekend travel is more family-oriented, with shopping, parks, meals, sports or activity stops, visits, and household errands. Timing is still bounded by family routines, but it is usually less tied to office hours.

The schedule should emphasize chained stops and timing pressure. Family anchors take priority over discretionary errands, and dwell times should reflect quick handoffs, longer activities, meals, shopping, and work blocks.
