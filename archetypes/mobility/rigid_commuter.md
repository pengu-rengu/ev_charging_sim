# Rigid Commuter Mobility

This person follows a predictable commute-centered routine. On weekdays, the day is anchored by a regular trip from home to work and back, with limited room for discretionary stops.

Typical stops include home, office, fast food or cafe stops near the commute path, supermarkets, pharmacies, gyms, banks, and occasional quick retail or household errands.

Weekday departures are usually consistent. The morning route tends to be direct, and the work dwell time is a long central block. After work, this person usually goes home or makes one practical stop that fits the route.

Weekend travel is less work-centered but still orderly. Shopping, errands, meals, gym visits, parks, or social plans can occur, but the day should not become overly scattered.

The schedule should be efficient and repeatable. Prefer direct routes, few stops, stable timing, and short-to-medium dwell times for errands outside the main work block.
